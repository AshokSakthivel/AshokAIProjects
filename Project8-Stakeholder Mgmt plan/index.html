§00 — Challenge

Stakeholder Mastery
Alignment at Executive Scale

Engineering influence is earned, not assumed. Without a deliberate stakeholder management strategy, critical decisions stall in email chains, executive sponsors lose confidence, and engineering roadmaps get hijacked by the loudest voice — not the best data. This plan builds trusted, measurable executive alignment across Product, Finance, Security, and Legal.

Ashok Sakthivel · Sr. Director of Engineering
Mimecast · UK / US / IN
April – June 2026
13 Key Stakeholders
⚠ Current State
  • Executive updates: ad-hoc email threads with no shared status view
  • Decisions stall >5 days — no async decision protocol with SLA
  • Product ↔ Engineering OKR alignment: 52% (measured last quarter)
  • Engineering roadmap often revised at sprint boundaries due to exec surprises
  • No stakeholder influence map — risk of misaligned investment decisions
  • Finance and Legal engaged reactively, not as proactive partners
⚡ Root Causes
  • No Cadence: Stakeholder reviews happen when problems arise, not proactively
  • Trust Gap: Engineering seen as execution arm, not strategic partner
  • Data Vacuum: Leaders make decisions without engineering metrics visibility
  • Escalation Reflex: Disagreements jump to VP level instead of resolving at team
  • Async Neglect: UK-IN timezone gap creates 24hr decision black holes
✅ Target State
  • Exec NPS for Engineering: ≥80 — leaders trust Ashok's delivery and judgment
  • Decision velocity: async decisions resolved in ≤48h with documented outcomes
  • OKR alignment: ≥95% Product + Engineering + Security shared objectives
  • Zero executive surprises: every stakeholder on a proactive cadence
  • Engineering = seat at the table in Product strategy, Finance planning, Security reviews
  • Escalation rate below 5% — issues resolved at the right level

Protocol 1

Async-First Decisions

All decisions >3 sentences documented in Confluence with a 48h SLA. Loom video for complex context requiring nuance. No decision in a meeting that doesn't have an async pre-read.

Protocol 2

Engineering 2-Pager Briefing

Fortnightly 2-page written briefing to exec sponsors: top 3 wins, top 2 risks, 1 investment ask. Replaces status meetings. Execs can consume async in 5 minutes with full context.

Protocol 3

PRFAQ for Scope Changes

Any scope change request to engineering roadmap must be submitted as a 1-page PRFAQ (Press Release / FAQ). Prevents verbal scope creep and ensures business justification is documented before engineering evaluates.

Protocol 4

3-Tier Escalation Framework

Tier 1: Team lead resolves in 24h. Tier 2: SD of Engineering resolves in 48h. Tier 3: VP+ only for unresolved strategic conflicts. Each tier tracks resolution time in the decision log.

§07 — Anti-Pattern Registry

Stakeholder Management Failure Modes

Anti-PatternSeverityRoot CauseMitigation
📢 Loudest Voice Wins
Decisions driven by seniority or volume rather than data — engineering roadmap overridden by the most vocal voice in the room
 High No data-driven decision culture; no shared metrics baseline; psychological safety deficit
Decision Log with mandatory data citation. PRFAQ for all scope changes. Ashok models data-first decision-making publicly in every exec review.
🤫 Status Update Black Hole
Executives receive no proactive status updates — they only hear from Engineering when something breaks or a deadline slips
 High No structured comms cadence; engineering assumes no news = good news
Fortnightly 2-pager brief + Loom for all exec sponsors. "No surprises" rule — any risk rated High must be flagged within 24h of identification.
🏃 Async Avoidance
Teams default to "we need a meeting" for every decision — timezone gaps create 24–48h stalls for cross-region decisions
 High No async decision protocol; fear that written decisions can be used against the sender
Async-first mandate. Confluence decision template with 48h SLA. Loom for context-heavy decisions. Psychological safety: async decisions are respected, not weaponised.
🎯 OKR Lip Service
OKRs written at quarter start but never referenced in sprint planning — alignment appears high on paper, zero in practice
 Medium OKRs owned by product, not co-authored with engineering; KRs not wired to sprint goals
Shared OKR authorship — Engineering co-writes KRs with Product. Sprint goals must map to an OKR KR. OKR progress reviewed fortnightly in Product ↔ Engineering sync.
🔥 Escalation as Default
Every cross-team disagreement is escalated to VP — leaders are bottlenecked, teams never develop conflict resolution skills
 Medium No escalation protocol; teams lack authority to resolve disagreements at their level
3-Tier Escalation Framework. Tier 1 disputes resolved without manager. Only Tier 3 reaches VP+. Escalation rate KPI published — high rate triggers coaching.
⏰ Timezone Debt
UK-IN overlap window is 4 hours/day — synchronous-only culture means decisions stall overnight, killing delivery velocity
 Medium No async protocol; teams not trusted to make decisions without real-time sign-off
Async Decision Log with 48h SLA + auto-escalation on breach. Overlap window reserved for strategic discussions only. Loom mandate for >3-paragraph context decisions.
🌀 Roadmap Volatility
Executive verbal requests bypass the product planning process — squads lose sprint focus and delivery estimates become meaningless
 Low No documented intake process for exec requests; fear of saying no to senior leaders
PRFAQ gates all scope change requests. Sprint commitments frozen 48h after sprint start. Executive requests queue in product backlog — triaged in next planning cycle.
