The GCI Reliability Framework

A phased operational mandate to transform 6 engineering teams from reactive firefighters into proactive reliability engineers. Define SLIs. Set SLOs. Build blameless culture. Survive chaos deliberately before it survives you accidentally.

⚡

The SRE Mandate — Why This Exists

Operational accountability is not optional at 60PB scale. Every minute of unplanned downtime on the ingestion pipeline violates data retention compliance for thousands of customers. Every search SLA breach during an active legal hold puts the organization at regulatory risk. We are moving from a culture where engineers react to pages to a culture where engineers design for failure.

This framework applies to all 6 domain teams. It is not a suggestion — it is the operating model. Teams without defined SLOs cannot argue for feature velocity. Teams with empty error budgets freeze features until reliability is restored. Reliability is the product.

Phased SRE Rollout — 4 Operational Maturity Phases

P0
Phase 0

Instrument Everything — "You Can't Fix What You Can't See"

Q1 2025 · 8 weeks · Foundation · All 6 Teams Required
✓ Complete
Strategic Focus
  • Achieve 100% observability coverage across all 6 domain services before any SLO is defined. You cannot set targets you cannot measure.
  • Deploy OpenTelemetry collectors to all EKS workloads. Instrument all Kafka consumers, S3 operations, and OpenSearch queries with distributed traces.
  • Stand up Grafana dashboards per team — every team must have a service health view before Phase 1.
  • Identify the top 5 "unobserved failure modes" per team through structured GameDay prep interviews.
SLI/SLO Boot Camp
  • Workshop: Every team selects 3–5 SLIs meaningful to their domain. SLIs must be measurable from existing telemetry within 48h of definition.
  • SLI candidates: request success rate, latency P99, throughput, error rate, data freshness lag, queue depth.
  • Draft SLO values are conservative: start at 99.5% — tighten as measurement baseline is established.
  • No SLA can be promised to customers for a domain whose SLO is not yet baselined for 30 days.
Foundation Toil Audit
  • Each team runs a 2-hour "Toil Sprint": catalog every manual, repetitive operational task they perform weekly.
  • Classify: Toil (automate), Overhead (streamline), or Genuine Engineering (keep).
  • Toil cap established: no team spends >20% of sprint capacity on toil. Violating teams get Platform Engineering (T6) embed.
  • Result: Toil register published in Backstage. Updated quarterly.
✓ OTel on 100% of services ✓ 18 SLIs defined across 6 teams ✓ Toil register published ✓ Grafana dashboards live per team
P1
Phase 1

Define & Enforce SLOs — "Set the Bar, Hold the Bar"

Q2 2025 · 10 weeks · SLO Governance · All 6 Teams
✓ Complete
SLO Ratification
  • All 6 teams present SLO proposals in a 2-hour "SLO Review" — Product, SRE Manager, and VP Architecture must sign off before SLOs are published.
  • SLOs are encoded in infrastructure as code: Terraform-managed CloudWatch alarms and Grafana alert rules enforce SLO burn rates automatically.
  • Error budget policy published: 100% budget consumed = feature freeze + SRE review board convenes within 48h.
  • SLOs are reviewed quarterly. Teams may propose tightening (never loosening without post-mortem justification).
PagerDuty On-Call Rotation
  • Each domain team establishes a formal on-call rotation: minimum 2 engineers per schedule, weekly rotation, no consecutive on-call without 48h gap.
  • On-Call Runbook required for every alert: alert name, expected blast radius, immediate mitigation steps, escalation path.
  • On-Call metrics tracked: pages per week, actionable vs. noisy, time spent per incident. Reported in weekly SRE standup.
  • Alert fatigue policy: any alert that doesn't require human action within 30 days is deleted. No alert cemetery in PagerDuty.
Error Budget Governance
  • Weekly error budget review in all team standups — SRE Manager attends T5 (Control Plane) due to accelerated burn.
  • Burn rate alerts: 2% hourly burn (1h window) triggers SEV-2 auto-escalation. 5% hourly burn triggers SEV-1.
  • Error budget spend on planned maintenance: teams may "spend" budget on chaos experiments — this is a feature, not a bug.
  • Unused budget at month-end: teams with >80% remaining receive "reliability dividend" — freed sprint capacity for technical debt.
✓ 18 SLOs ratified across 6 teams ✓ PagerDuty rotations live ✓ Error budget policy enforced
P2
Phase 2

Incident Mastery & Blameless Culture — "Own Your Blast Radius" 🔵 ACTIVE

Q3 2025 – Q2 2026 · On-going · Incident Maturity
● In Progress
Incident Command System
  • Tiered incident protocol live: SEV-1 through SEV-4 with defined MTTA/MTTR SLOs per tier (see Incident Tiers below).
  • Incident Commander (IC) role rotates through senior engineers across teams — not permanently assigned to SRE. Every team owns incidents in their domain.
  • War Room protocol: SEV-1/SEV-2 auto-creates #inc-{{timestamp}} Slack channel, provisions Zoom bridge, and pages IC within 60 seconds.
  • Incident Commander has final authority during active SEV-1: can make unilateral architectural rollback decisions without approval chain.
Blameless Post-Mortem (COE)
  • Every SEV-1 and SEV-2 requires a Correction of Error (COE) document published within 5 business days.
  • COE structure: Timeline (UTC timestamps), contributing factors (5-Whys), blast radius assessment, customer impact, immediate mitigations applied, and Action Items with owners + due dates.
  • COE review meeting: 45-min blameless retro with all teams involved. SRE Manager facilitates. No naming individuals in public COE without consent.
  • Action Items are SMART: Specific, Measurable, Assigned, Realistic, Time-bound. Tracked in the Post-Mortem Ledger — visible to VP Architecture weekly.
Continuous Improvement
  • Weekly SRE Sync (30 min): Top 3 reliability signals, error budget burn status, overdue COE actions flagged.
  • Monthly Reliability Review: VP Architecture reviews MTTA/MTTR trends, team-by-team SLO performance, and top toil sources.
  • Reliability KPIs published in the GCI Operations Hub — visible to all 60 engineers. Transparency creates accountability.
  • COE Action Item closure rate target: 90% within due date. Teams below 80% enter SRE Reliability Improvement Plan (RIP).
🔵 IC rotation live across 6 teams 🔵 COE process: 12 docs published YTD 🔵 Action closure rate: 82% (target 90%)

Phase 3

Chaos Engineering & Proactive Resilience — "Break It Before It Breaks You"

Q3 2026 → Continuous · Advanced Resilience · Game Days
Planned Q3 2026
Game Day Program
  • Quarterly Game Days: full-team simulated failure scenarios in production-equivalent staging environment. Mandatory for T1, T2, T4 (direct compliance impact).
  • Scenarios: S3 bucket ACL misconfiguration; Kafka broker failure; OpenSearch cluster split-brain; Glacier restore under e-discovery deadline; full region failover drill.
  • Game Day report required within 48h: what broke, what held, what runbooks need updating, error budget impact of deliberate failures.
  • Results feed into next-quarter SLO review. Consistently-failing scenarios get reliability sprint allocation.
AWS Fault Injection Service
  • AWS FIS experiments codified in Terraform and scheduled monthly. Automatically target a random 15% of EKS nodes for CPU overload.
  • FIS experiments run during business hours only — never on-call hours. Engineers must observe blast radius live, not discover from alerts.
  • Chaos hypotheses documented before each experiment: "We believe the ingestion pipeline will continue processing at >90% during a single Kafka broker failure."
  • Hypothesis validation tracked in the Post-Mortem Ledger alongside reactive COEs — proactive and reactive share the same accountability surface.
Reliability Automation Targets
  • SLO burn auto-remediation: build Lambda runbooks that automatically roll back the last deployment when 2% hourly error budget burn is detected.
  • Self-healing Kafka consumer lag: ECS task count auto-scales when consumer lag exceeds 50,000 messages — no human paging for predictable load events.
  • Automated compliance checks: daily Lambda validates WORM compliance across 100% of new S3 objects — pages T4 if any object escapes Object Lock.
  • Toil target: <10% of team sprint capacity on toil by end of Phase 3 (down from 20% cap in Phase 0).
Target: 4 Game Days/year per high-risk team Target: <10% toil per team Target: 80% of runbooks automated

Incident Response Protocol — Severity Tiers

SEV-1
Total Outage / Compliance Break
Complete service unavailability OR WORM integrity violated OR legal hold failed across ANY tenant. Regulatory SLA at risk.

Examples: Kafka broker complete loss; OpenSearch unavailable; S3 Object Lock misconfigured on new objects; legal hold fail-to-apply.
MTTA: <2 min MTTR: <15 min COE: Mandatory Comms: <5 min
SEV-2
Significant Degradation
Partial service degradation affecting >10% of tenants. SLO burn rate >2%/hr. Search latency exceeds SLO for active e-discovery cases.

Examples: OpenSearch index lag >30min; API P99 >3× SLO; Glacier restore queue saturation during active litigation.
MTTA: <5 min MTTR: <30 min COE: Required Comms: <15 min
SEV-3
Minor Degradation
Non-critical component degraded. <10% of tenants impacted. No SLA breach imminent. Workarounds exist.

Examples: Consumer lag elevated >5,000 msgs; cost anomaly alert; single connector failure; DLQ depth growing.
MTTA: <15 min MTTR: <2 hrs COE: Optional
SEV-4
Operational Notice
Informational alert. No user impact. Proactive observation requiring investigation during business hours.

Examples: Untagged S3 objects; certificate expiry >30 days out; cost optimization opportunity identified.
MTTA: Next business day No COE

Incident Response Roles

🎯
Incident Commander (IC)
Rotates through all domain team Staff/Senior Engineers — not an SRE-only role
  • Declares severity level within 2 minutes of page receipt
  • Owns the incident timeline — defines what is in scope and explicitly out of scope
  • Has unilateral authority to trigger a rollback or initiate DR — no approval chain during SEV-1
  • Delegates investigation to domain engineers; does not investigate themselves during active incident
  • Calls the "clear" when incident is resolved — owns the 5-business-day COE deadline
  • Updates Slack war room every 15 minutes during SEV-1, every 30 minutes during SEV-2
🔧
On-Call Engineer
Domain team member. First responder for their bounded context
  • Acknowledges PagerDuty page within MTTA SLO for their team's severity tier
  • Posts initial assessment to Slack war room within 5 minutes of ack: "What I see, what I've ruled out, what I'm doing next"
  • Executes runbook steps — escalates to IC if runbook doesn't resolve within 10 minutes
  • Does NOT attempt heroic solo fixes without IC awareness during SEV-1/SEV-2
  • Owns the technical timeline entries in the COE document
  • Completes on-call handoff checklist at end of shift: open issues, monitoring anomalies, pending actions
📣
Communications Lead (Comms)
Dedicated role during SEV-1/SEV-2 — separate from IC and On-Call
  • Posts customer-facing status page update within 5 minutes of SEV-1 declaration (even if "investigating")
  • Drafts and sends internal executive briefing at 15 minutes for any SEV-1 — VP Engineering and Legal must be looped for compliance incidents
  • Manages #inc-* Slack channel visibility — pins status updates, links to runbooks, removes noise
  • Coordinates cross-team stakeholder communication — T4 Compliance must be looped on any storage-related SEV-1
  • Publishes "All Clear" notification with resolution summary to internal status page
  • Owns the "customer impact" section of the COE document

Continuous Improvement — COE & Game Day Framework

📋
Blameless Post-Mortem (COE Document)
Correction of Error · Required for SEV-1 and SEV-2 · Due within 5 business days
  • Section 1 — Impact Summary: Customer impact (# tenants, data affected), duration (UTC start to clear), regulatory implications if any.
  • Section 2 — Timeline: Minute-by-minute UTC event log from first signal to resolution. No passive voice. No pronouns. "The cache miss caused X" not "Someone misconfigured Y."
  • Section 3 — Root Cause Analysis: 5-Whys technique applied. Primary contributing factor + compounding factors. Systemic issues vs. one-offs distinguished.
  • Section 4 — What Went Well: Blameless means celebrating what worked. Runbooks that fired correctly. Alerts that triggered. Engineers who escalated correctly.
  • Section 5 — Action Items: Minimum 3 action items per SEV-1. Each has: owner (named individual), due date, and verification method. JIRA ticket linked.
  • Section 6 — Error Budget Impact: How many minutes/hours of SLO were consumed. Impact on current month's error budget per team.
  • COE Review Meeting: 45 minutes, facilitated by SRE Manager, recorded but not shared outside team. No blame language permitted — facilitator corrects immediately.
  • COE published in Backstage. Searchable by teams. "We've seen this before" is a failure of our system, not people.

Game Day & Chaos Engineering Program
Quarterly Game Days · AWS FIS Monthly Experiments · Phase 3 (Q3 2026)
  • Purpose: Failure is inevitable. Practice makes it boring. Every Game Day converts a surprise into a rehearsed drill. Engineers who run the drill own the runbook.
  • Game Day Format (Half-Day): 1h scenario briefing → 2h live failure drill in staging → 30min immediate debrief → 48h written report. Teams rotate ownership each quarter.
  • Mandatory Scenarios — T1 Ingestion: Single Kafka broker failure; simulate 3× normal ingest load (Black Friday test); SMTP connector cert expiry.
  • Mandatory Scenarios — T2 Search: OpenSearch node failure under active e-discovery query load; dual-write pipeline failure mid-migration; index rebuild trigger during peak search.
  • Mandatory Scenarios — T4 Compliance: Legal hold apply during S3 outage; Glacier restore SLA test for 10-year-old data; WORM Object Lock bypass attempt (verify it fails).
  • AWS FIS Monthly Experiments: CPU stress on 15% of EKS nodes; inject Kafka broker latency (200ms artificial delay); throttle S3 PUT rate by 50%; inject Lambda timeout.
  • Hypothesis-Driven Chaos: All experiments start with a written hypothesis. "Under X failure, we expect Y behavior with Z outcome." Invalidated hypotheses are immediate action items.
  • Budget for Chaos: Game Days intentionally consume error budget. This is planned spend — teams notify SRE Manager 5 business days before executing. Budget protected by policy.
