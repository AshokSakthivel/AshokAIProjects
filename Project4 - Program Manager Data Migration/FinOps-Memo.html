§ 01
The Vision — Why This Matters Now
Strategic

We are executing one of the most complex AWS migrations this organization has undertaken. Every architectural decision we make — instance sizing, data transfer paths, storage tier selection, query optimization — carries an immediate and compounding financial impact on our Cost of Goods Sold (COGS).

As we accelerate toward the May 31st Go-Live milestone and the June 30th Maintenance Window, we cannot afford to discover cost anomalies retroactively during a monthly billing cycle. We are shifting left on cloud cost management, embedding financial accountability directly into our daily engineering cadence.

This is not a finance initiative. This is an engineering excellence initiative with a dollar sign attached.

§ 03
The Rule — 15% Daily Spike Alert Protocol
Mandatory
If any team's total infrastructure cost increases by 15% or more day-over-day, an automated alert is triggered and escalated to the team's designated FinOps champion and their engineering lead.

Why 15%? This threshold is calibrated to catch real anomalies — not routine deployment fluctuations — while filtering out expected scaling events. A 15% single-day spike typically signals one of the following:

🖥️
Compute SprawlOrphaned instances, over-provisioned autoscaling, test environments left running.
🌐
Egress AnomaliesUnexpected cross-region or cross-AZ data transfer costs.
🔍
Inefficient QueriesRunaway scans on RDS, Redshift, or DynamoDB consuming excess I/O.
🏷️
Untagged ResourcesInfrastructure deployed outside the approved IaC pipeline.

✓ This is not punitive. A spike alert is a rapid-response mechanism — not a performance review trigger. The goal is to catch, investigate, and resolve cost inefficiencies within 24 hours before they compound.

Severity Trigger Response SLA
⚠ Warning 15–24% day-over-day spike Acknowledge + root cause within 24 hours
🔴 Critical ≥ 25% day-over-day spike Acknowledge + mitigation plan within 4 hours
