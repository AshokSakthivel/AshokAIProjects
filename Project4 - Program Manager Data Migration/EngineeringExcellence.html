§ 00 · FOUNDATION
The Problem We're Solving
Strategic Context

The Micromanagement Trap vs. The Measurement Gap. Engineering organizations fail in one of two ways: either they have no visibility into quality signals and fly blind, or they instrument everything and create a surveillance culture that destroys psychological safety. This initiative avoids both failure modes.

THE OLD WAY ✗
  • Gut-feel code reviews with no baseline
  • JIRA velocity as the only metric
  • Post-incident blame, not systemic learning
  • Toil accumulates silently until burnout
  • Tech debt as a feeling, not a cost
THE NEW WAY ✓
  • DORA metrics as shared organizational health signals
  • PR cycle time as a flow bottleneck indicator
  • Blameless postmortems feeding the SEI database
  • Toil budget enforced via SLO + alert fatigue tracking
  • Tech debt as a quantified sprint capacity cost
OWNERSHIP MODEL
  • Federated: each Tech Lead owns their team's data
  • Aggregate: Ashok sees org-wide trends, not individuals
  • Pull, not push: TLs pull insights for retrospectives
  • Shield, not sword: metrics used to remove blockers
  • Quarterly excellence reviews, not daily surveillance

§ 02 · EXCELLENCE ROADMAP
The 90-Day + Q2 Execution Plan
Click Phases to Expand
0–30 DAYS
Phase 1 — Instrument & Baseline · Days 0–30 Apr 1 – Apr 30, 2026 Foundation ▶

Behavioral Shift: Move from "we assume quality is good" to "we have a factual baseline." No optimization without measurement. This phase is observation-only — no mandates, no targets, just data collection and calibration.

Quality & Architecture Levers
  • Deploy standardized PR templates across all repos — sections: Description, Testing Evidence, Risk Assessment, Rollback Plan
  • Instrument GitHub/GitLab webhooks into SEI data lake — capture PR open/merge timestamps, review comment counts, CI pass/fail
  • Audit current test coverage per service — jest --coverage, pytest-cov, or equivalent; establish actuals without judgment
  • Identify the top-3 most complex modules per team using cyclomatic complexity tools (radon, sonarqube)
  • Establish ADR (Architecture Decision Record) format — retroactively document the top-5 most impactful past decisions per team
Data & Measurement Strategy
  • Stand up DORA metrics pipeline — connect deployment events from CI/CD to a central data store (BigQuery / Redshift)
  • Capture baseline: Deployment Frequency, Lead Time, MTTR, Change Failure Rate — no targets yet, just actuals
  • Instrument PR Cycle Time: opened → first review → approved → merged (4 sub-stages exposed separately)
  • Begin Code Churn tracking — identify files with >3 revisions in 30 days (indicator of unclear requirements or unstable interfaces)
  • Instrument PagerDuty / OpsGenie alert volumes per team — pager fatigue baseline, MTTA (Mean Time to Acknowledge)
Federated Ownership Actions
  • Each Tech Lead runs a Day 15 Calibration Session — review raw data with team; discuss what looks wrong before acting
  • Establish team-specific Engineering Excellence Backlog in Jira/Linear — quality work is tracked like feature work
  • Create shared #eng-excellence Slack channel — TLs post weekly SEI snapshot; celebrate improvements, not just warn on regressions
  • Nominate Quality Champions per team (1 engineer each) — hands-on partner to the TL for measurement and improvement work
31–60 DAYS
Phase 2 — Quality Gates & Standards · Days 31–60 May 1 – May 31, 2026 Quality Enforcement ▶

Behavioral Shift: Shift from "quality review is a human judgment" to "quality gates are automated guardrails." Engineers get rapid, objective feedback from the pipeline itself — not from a code review comment two days later. Shift-left on security, reliability, and architectural integrity.

Quality & Architecture Levers
  • Enforce SonarQube / CodeClimate quality gates in CI — block merge if code smell score regresses, coverage drops >5%, or critical security issues found
  • Deploy architectural linting via ArchUnit (Java) or import-linter (Python) — enforce layer isolation rules as code
  • Implement shift-left security scanning: SAST via Semgrep or CodeQL in every PR pipeline; secrets detection via Trufflehog
  • Standardize golden CI/CD pipeline templates — teams adopt the Golden Path rather than building bespoke pipelines (reduces toil + inconsistency)
  • Enforce dependency vulnerability scanning on every build — Dependabot or Snyk with SLA-gated merge blocking for CRITICAL CVEs
Data & Measurement Strategy
  • Set first DORA baseline targets per team — not org-wide mandates; each team sets their own 30-day improvement targets based on their baseline
  • Track review thoroughness scores: comment-to-line-ratio, percentage of PRs merged with zero review comments (auto-merge risk flag)
  • Instrument test flakiness — flaky tests erode trust in CI; track flake rate per suite; gate new flaky tests from merging
  • Introduce Tech Debt Index: SonarQube debt minutes + churn rate + test gap combined score, surfaced weekly per team
  • Begin incident attribution — link production incidents back to the deployment that caused them; feed into Change Failure Rate calculation
Federated Ownership Actions
  • Each TL publishes a Team Excellence Scorecard — distributed to their team every 2 weeks, reviewed in sprint retros
  • First cross-team excellence sync — TLs share their most impactful quality improvement and one persisting blocker; no ranking or comparison
  • Launch Golden Path adoption sprint — each team migrates one service to the standardized CI/CD pipeline template
  • Introduce "15% time for quality" — sprint capacity policy: 15% of engineering capacity reserved for quality debt, test coverage, and tooling
61–90 DAYS
Phase 3 — Automation & Operational Reliability · Days 61–90 Jun 1 – Jun 30, 2026 Reliability Engineering ▶

Behavioral Shift: Move from "we react to incidents" to "we proactively validate resilience." SLOs are owned by teams, not imposed from above. Chaos engineering graduates from fear-inducing to routine hygiene. MTTR drops because runbooks are automated, not because engineers are faster.

Quality & Architecture Levers
  • Define and publish SLOs per service — availability, latency p99, error rate targets in YAML alongside service code (SLOs as code)
  • Deploy Chaos Engineering Basics — adopt Chaos Monkey or Gremlin Lite; begin with non-prod kill tests; escalate to scheduled prod game days
  • Automate runbook-driven incident response — PagerDuty runbook links in alerts; first-runbook-action must be executable, not a wiki link
  • Enforce Feature Flags on all production changes — decouple deploy from release; enables immediate rollback without redeploy
  • Implement contract testing for service interfaces — Pact or Spring Cloud Contract; catch breaking changes before integration
Data & Measurement Strategy
  • Track MTTR trends weekly — not as a judgment but as a signal: increasing MTTR → insufficient runbooks or missing observability; decreasing → automation working
  • Measure SLO burn rate (error budget consumption) per service — surface in SEI portal as traffic-light indicator
  • Deploy full-stack distributed tracing (OpenTelemetry) — connect user-facing latency to specific service call chains; enables surgical performance debugging
  • Quantify toil hours per on-call cycle — manual tasks that could be automated; TL tracks and commits to reducing by 20% per quarter
  • Run the first SEI Executive Review — Ashok presents org-wide trends to leadership; no individual team data exposed upward, only aggregate
Federated Ownership Actions
  • Each TL leads a Game Day / Chaos Drill with their team — document outcomes, feed findings into ADR repository
  • TLs conduct quarterly architecture health reviews — evaluate coupling metrics, dependency graph changes, Golden Path adoption %
  • Launch Excellence Champions Program — rotating 3-month appointment; champion leads one org-wide improvement initiative (test framework, observability tooling, etc.)
  • Publish first Engineering Excellence Annual Report summary — before/after DORA state; shared at all-hands as a team success story
Q2+ CULTURE
Phase 4 — The Culture Flywheel · Q2 2026 and Beyond Jul 1, 2026 → Continuous Sustained Excellence ▶

Behavioral Shift: Engineering Excellence is no longer an initiative — it's how we work. Quality practices are self-enforcing through tooling, culture, and career incentives. The flywheel: better data → clearer blockers → faster resolution → better data.

Sustained Quality Practices
  • DORA Elite Certification — teams achieving Elite-tier across all 4 metrics get publicly recognized and serve as internal mentors
  • Annual Architecture Review Board — cross-team review of ADR library, deprecated tech stack decisions, new Golden Path patterns
  • Tech Debt Quarterly Burn-Down — formal capacity allocation reviewed at portfolio planning; debt is a first-class product concern
  • Integrate SEI metrics into hiring and onboarding — new engineers shown team's health dashboard; excellence standards set before day one
Advanced SEI Capabilities
  • ML-assisted PR risk scoring — train model on historical change-failure data; surface high-risk PRs for deeper review automatically
  • Developer Experience (DevEx) Index — quarterly survey + objective metrics (build times, flake rates, onboarding cycle time) combined
  • Inner Source contribution tracking — measure cross-team code contributions; incentivize platform thinking over silo ownership
  • Cost-of-Quality dashboard — incident resolution cost + rework time quantified in engineering-hours per quarter
Career & Culture Integration
  • Excellence metrics embedded in IC promotion criteria — "owning quality improvement" is a promotable behavior, explicitly documented
  • Blameless postmortem culture codified — incident review template mandated; finding systemic causes = career positive, not negative
  • Engineering blog publication — each team publishes one technical post per quarter; builds external employer brand + internal knowledge transfer
  • Annual Engineering Excellence Summit — full-day internal conference; teams present their most impactful quality improvement stories

§ 05 · GOLDEN PATH REPOSITORY
Architectural Standards & Approved Tooling Compendium
Standards Authority
⚙️ CI/CD GOLDEN PIPELINE
  • Stage 1: lint + format check (fail-fast <30s)
  • Stage 2: unit + integration tests (parallel, <5min)
  • Stage 3: SAST + secrets scan (Semgrep + Trufflehog)
  • Stage 4: container build + SBOM (Syft + Grype)
  • Stage 5: quality gate (SonarQube; block on regress)
  • Stage 6: deploy to staging + smoke tests
  • Stage 7: canary + feature flag controlled prod release
📦 APPROVED TECH STACK
  • Runtime: Node.js LTS, Python 3.12, Java 21 (LTS)
  • Containers: Docker + Helm 3; OCI-compliant images only
  • Observability: OpenTelemetry → Datadog / Grafana stack
  • Service Mesh: Istio (approved); direct HTTP only with ADR
  • API: REST + OpenAPI 3.1 mandatory; gRPC for internal only
  • Auth: OAuth 2.0 + OIDC; no custom auth schemes
  • DB: PostgreSQL 16 (primary); DynamoDB (key-value); Redis (cache)
🚫 ANTI-PATTERN REGISTRY
  • ▸ Shared mutable database between services (distributed monolith)
  • ▸ Synchronous HTTP chains deeper than 3 hops (cascade failure risk)
  • ▸ Secrets in environment variables without vault injection
  • ▸ latest Docker image tags in any staging or production manifest
  • ▸ Test mocking of internal implementation (test to interface, not impl)
  • ▸ Monolithic test suites >10 min without parallelization strategy
  • ▸ Architecture changes without an ADR entry in repository

The MTTR Mask
Closing incidents fast by reverting, not fixing root cause		 MTTR improves; incident recurrence rate stays high; same services page repeatedly CRITICAL Mandatory blameless postmortem with "fix or accept" decision; track recurring incident rate separately from MTTR TL + SRE
Hero Culture Dependency
One engineer "saves" every incident or release		 Pager alerts concentrated on 1–2 engineers; deployer diversity <30%; bus factor = 1 per service HIGH Rotating on-call enforcement; pair deployments; bus factor tracked as first-class SEI metric Tech Lead
Metric Gaming
Deploying trivial changes to inflate deployment frequency		 Deployment frequency spikes; PR size drops to <5 lines on average; no associated feature JIRA tickets MEDIUM Track meaningful deployment ratio (PRs linked to user-value tickets); DORA treated as system signal, not individual KPI Ashok + TL
Coverage Theater
High coverage % with low-value assertion-free tests		 Coverage >80% but CFR remains high; mutation testing score low; incident post-mortems cite "missing test for this path" MEDIUM Introduce mutation testing score (PiTest, Mutmut) alongside line coverage; enforce test quality, not just quantity Quality Champion
ADR Amnesia
Architecture decisions made but never recorded; patterns diverge silently		 ADR library stale (>6 months without new entry); new services diverge from approved tech stack; Golden Path adoption <60% MEDIUM ADR creation required in PR template for any new service or technology introduction; quarterly ADR review in Architecture sync Tech Lead
Toil Accumulation
Manual on-call tasks grow faster than automation capacity		 Pager Fatigue Index rising quarter-over-quarter; on-call rotation declining (engineers opting out); toil hours >30% of on-call cycle HIGH 15% of sprint capacity mandatory for toil reduction; each toil task documented and assigned an automation owner; track toil-hours closed per quarter TL + SRE
